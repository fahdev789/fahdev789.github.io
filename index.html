// research direction

What I'm actually working on

active

Interpretability

"If a model is doing something concerning internally, we should be able to point to where, without relying on its output alone."

Mechanistic and representation-level methods for surfacing what a model is actually representing — with an eye toward tools a non-specialist team could actually run.

active

Evaluations & red-teaming

"An eval is only as trustworthy as the failure modes it was designed to miss."

Building and breaking evaluation suites for dangerous capabilities, with a focus on adversarial coverage rather than benchmark scores.

exploring

Governance & policy interfaces

"Technical findings only matter if they reach the people deciding what ships."

Translating interpretability and eval results into artifacts — safety cases, model cards, incident reports — that policy and governance teams can actually use.
